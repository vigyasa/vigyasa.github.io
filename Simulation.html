Simulations

Called as the "The Great Explainer", Feyman and his books are your best bets to dwelve and understand physics to the core because of the simplicity of his explanations and the unity of the book.So as to extend on this, Shivam and I created certain visualizations to simplify certain topics and explain them more accurately to other students. Using these simulations, we were able to inculcate an excitement for Physics amongst children and students and also help them understand that topic better.

Drag it- Gravity Ball

A simulation as simple as a bouncing ball, this is intended to simplify and help understand the concepts of gravity, air-drag and co-efficient of restitution. The values of rho and 'e' can be altered as given below the simulation.

  • Try it!

Spring-Mass System

Behaviour of a spring-mass system, terms like resonant frequency are known theoretically but usually, it's hard to understand the practical meaning of these terms. Hence, this simulation of the Hooke's Law helps in clearing those fundamentals.

  • Try it!

Interference

Anyone who says that he understands quantum physics is either lying or is crazy!
Feynman said this back in the 1960's and it holds true but with the advent of computers and Javascript, you never know.So to challenge that statement, here is a simulation that 'helps you understand' how waves interfere.
